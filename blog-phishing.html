What is Phishing and How to Protect Yourself

Published on: October 2023

Phishing is a type of cyberattack where attackers impersonate legitimate entities (e.g., banks, companies) to trick users into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, credit card numbers, or Social Security numbers.

How Phishing Works

Attackers often use emails, fake websites, or malicious links to deceive victims. For example:

  • You receive an email claiming to be from your bank asking you to "verify" your account details.
  • You click a link that takes you to a fake website designed to look like your bank's login page.
  • You enter your credentials, which are then stolen by the attacker.

How to Protect Yourself

To avoid falling victim to phishing attacks:

  1. Always verify the sender's email address before clicking links.
  2. Hover over links to check their actual destination.
  3. Never share sensitive information via email or unsecured websites.
  4. Enable multi-factor authentication (MFA) on your accounts.
